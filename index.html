Processing Details

International users, please contact us FIRST before ordering.

1. We receive your shoes.

Preferably clean (dirty shoes get lower grades). No boxes. TSS’s receiving department opens newly arrived packages each morning and immediately verifies that the number of shoes in each package matches the number shown on the invoice. Once this is done, a more detailed comparison is made to ensure that their invoice descriptions correspond to the actual shoes. This information is traceable at all stages of the grading process by the invoice number. Before any grading is performed, each pair is examined by a restoration detection professional. If any form of restoration work is detected, this information is noted so that it will be available to the grading team.

2. We inspect and grade your shoes.

Using our 27-point inspection process, any peculiarities or flaws that may affect a shoe’s grade are tracked. Some examples of this would be “significant toe box creasing,” “scuffs on leather,” “a ¼” inch scratch,” and so forth. When the grader examines the shoes, he is not able to see a previously assigned grade, so as to not influence his own evaluation.

3. We package and register your shoes.

After each pair has been graded and the necessary numbers and text entered into their respective data fields, all pairs from a particular order are taken from the Grading Department into the Packaging Department. Here, appropriately color-coded tamper-evident holographic labels are printed out bearing the appropriate grade and serial number. This last item is extremely important, as it serves to make each certified pair unique and is also an important deterrent to counterfeiting TSS's valued product. All of the relevant information is tracked in our database.

4. We send your shoes along to their next destination.

After packaging, all shoes go through a quality control inspection. Here, they are examined to make certain that their labels are correct for both the grade and the serial number. Quality control also inspects each case for any flaws, such as scuffs or nicks. While these are quite rare, TSS is careful to make certain that the shoes we grade are not only accurately graded, but attractively presented as well. No matter whether the US Postal Service or some private carrier is used, the method of packaging is essentially the same.The encapsulated shoes are placed inside boxes made of very sturdy cardboard.

Notices regarding TSS’s Census Report

The utilization of this report as a tool for assessing the population and value of sneakers in any character or grade is unreliable. The following characteristics inherent in the marketplace undermine the accuracy of this report:

  • Inexpensive sneakers, which are not generally submitted for certification, may appear scarce but are not
  • Sneaker grading services are predominantly utilized for higher-grade sneakers
  • Certified sneakers are often removed from their holders without notifying the grading service; therefore, computer tallies utilized to provide population reports may be misleading
  • Rarity is only one factor that must be weighed in determining the market value of a pair of sneakers

TSS encourages all collectors to seek the counsel of qualified professionals familiar with the certified sneaker marketplace before making any purchase based on this report.
